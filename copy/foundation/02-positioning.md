# 02 — Positioning

The keystone. Everything downstream — messaging, taglines, every channel kit —
is derived from this. If we change this, we change everything. If we get it
right, the rest writes itself.

---

## The positioning statement

> **For** busy households tired of watching groceries — and the money they spent
> on them — quietly go to waste,
> **ZeroSpoils** is the food-tracking app that scans what you buy and reminds you
> to use it before it's gone.
> **Unlike** sticky notes, mental lists, and good intentions,
> **ZeroSpoils** runs quietly in the background and never forgets — so nothing
> good, and none of your money, ends up in the bin.

---

## The one-line spine

**ZeroSpoils turns the food you forget into money you keep.**

(Everything we say should ladder back to this sentence.)

---

## The building blocks

**Category (what people search for):** a food-tracking / kitchen-inventory app —
expiry reminders, what's-in-my-fridge.

**Brand category (how we want to be understood):** not a chore app, not a
budgeting app — *a money-saving habit that lives in your kitchen.*

**Target (who, in priority order):**
1. The Stretched Household — feels every grocery dollar; the mass-market core.
2. The Optimizer — runs life on systems; our earliest evangelists.
3. The Climate-Conscious — values-driven; our loudest sharers.
4. The Overwhelmed Organizer — wants it handled, automatically.

**Frame of reference (the alternative we replace):** the status quo — memory,
sticky notes, a deep fridge, and the hope you'll get to it in time. The quiet
bin is what we're up against, not a rival app.

**Point of difference (what's uniquely true):** ZeroSpoils captures groceries in
seconds — scan a barcode or a whole receipt — then does the remembering *for*
you, surfacing what to use first. The effort is near zero; the system never
forgets. That combination is the wedge.

**Reasons to believe (the proof):**
- Barcode + full-receipt + fresh-produce OCR → a shop logged in seconds, not
  minutes of typing.
- Smart expiry alerts with adjustable lead time → the nudge arrives while the
  food is still good.
- Zesto, the in-app guide → storage tips that make food last longer.
- Offline-first and privacy-first → it just works, and your data stays yours.

## What we are / what we're not

| We are | We're not |
|--------|-----------|
| A money-saving habit | A guilt trip about the planet |
| Effortless capture (scan it) | A manual food diary / spreadsheet |
| The thing that remembers for you | Another to-do list to maintain |
| On the user's side, vs. forgetting | A scold about being wasteful |
| Quiet and automatic | Loud, gamified, or naggy |

---

## Why this wins

The cost-of-living squeeze makes "stop binning money" the most universal hook in
North America right now — far broader than "save the planet." But the planet
gives the brand a soul that a pure budgeting tool lacks, earning loyalty and
word of mouth once people are in. Leading with money and following with meaning
captures the widest door *and* the deepest reason to stay. The status-quo enemy
keeps us out of a feature war and lets us define the category on our terms.
