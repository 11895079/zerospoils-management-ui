# 06 — Naming & Taglines

The verbal system. A hero line we own, supporting lines per context, pitches at
three lengths, the press boilerplate, and the rules for naming things (including
Zesto). All in the warm voice (`03`), all laddering to the spine.

> **Spine:** ZeroSpoils remembers your groceries, so you don't waste them.

---

## Hero tagline

**Locked:**
> ## Make every grocery count.

Warm, money-leaning, and honest to the mechanism: every grocery you buy gets
used, not binned. Works in a logo lock-up, an ad, or said aloud. Pairs with a
money-explicit line when a cold context needs the hook spelled out (below).

**Alternatives (on the bench):**
- *Good food, never forgotten.* (warm, tight; mechanism-first)
- *Nothing good goes to waste.* (warm, brand-y; food + planet)
- *Forget nothing. Waste nothing.* (punchy, parallel)
- *Stop binning money.* (sharpest money hook — more edge than warm)

**Retired (with reason):**
- *Use it. Don't lose it.* — "use it or lose it" is a worn idiom owned by
  fitness/memory; borrowed, not ownable, wrong association.
- *Your fridge has a memory now.* — risks smart-fridge category confusion
  (Samsung Family Hub, LG InstaView) and me-too positioning.

---

## Supporting line system

Use the hero for brand; use these where the context needs clarity or the hook:

| Context | Line |
|---------|------|
| Money-explicit hook (ads, paid) | "The groceries you forget are money in the bin. ZeroSpoils makes sure you don't." |
| App Store subtitle (clarity + keywords) | "Track expiry dates. Waste less. Save money." |
| Social bio | "The app that remembers your groceries, so good food (and your money) doesn't go to waste. 🌍" |
| One-liner for intros | "ZeroSpoils scans your groceries and reminds you to use them before they're gone." |
| Planet/loyalty moment | "Every meal you don't waste is a small win — for your wallet and the planet." |

## Elevator pitches

**10 words:**
> ZeroSpoils reminds you to use groceries before they're wasted.

**25 words:**
> ZeroSpoils is a food-tracking app that scans your groceries and reminds you to
> use them before they expire — saving you money and cutting waste, effortlessly.

**50 words:**
> Good food goes to waste in busy homes because it's easy to lose track.
> ZeroSpoils fixes that: scan a barcode or a whole receipt, and it quietly
> reminds you to use things before they're gone. Less money in the bin, less
> guilt, less waste — without changing how you live.

## Talking about Zesto

Zesto is the friendly in-app guide — the helpful-friend personality made
concrete. Rules:
- Zesto **helps and suggests**; it never nags or scolds (mirrors brand voice).
- Introduce as *"Zesto, your food sidekick"* — not "AI assistant" or "chatbot."
- Zesto's tips are practical and warm: storage tricks, what-to-use-first, simple
  ideas. Short. Never preachy.

---

## Feature-naming ground rules

- **Plain over clever.** Name features for what they do: "Receipt Scan," "Expiry
  Reminders," "Saved this week." A user should never need a glossary.
- **No internal jargon in the UI.** "Reference packs," "OCR," "telemetry" stay
  backstage.
- **"ZeroSpoils" is the only literal brand name** that never varies. Publisher
  identity is always a token.
- **Pro tier:** name the *benefit*, not the paywall — e.g. "ZeroSpoils Plus" with
  value framing, never "premium upgrade required."
