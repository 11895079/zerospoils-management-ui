## Part 1: Clarifying the Relationship: Co-Founder vs. Advisor

### The Key Distinction

**Co-Founder Model** (Y Combinator's "equal split" article):
- Multiple people founding a company **together from inception**
- Each takes on **equal execution risk and responsibility**
- Each designs the product and company strategy
- Example: "Alice, Bob, Charlie all quit their jobs on Day 1 to start Company X"
- Equity split: Often 33/33/33 (or adjusted for different roles)
- **Reference:** [Y Combinator. "How to Split Equity Among Co-Founders"](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/5x-how-to-split-equity-among-co-founders)

**Advisor Model** (non-executing strategic input):
- One person founds and executes alone
- Others join later to provide ideation, feedback, market insight
- No execution responsibility; no ongoing operational risk
- Example: "You built the MVP solo. Your friends meet weekly to brainstorm direction."
- Equity split: Typically 0.5–2% per advisor (vs. 90%+ for founder)
- **Reference:** [AngelList. "What percentage of equity should I give to startup advisors?"](https://angel.co/blog/what-percentage-of-equity-should-i-give-to-startup-advisors)

### ZeroSpoils: Which Model?

**The Reality:**
- ✅ You founded ZeroSpoils solo (before collaborators were involved)
- ✅ You executed 227/227 commits, wrote all code, defined all strategy
- ✅ Collaborators joined after MVP direction was set
- ✅ They've provided weekly ideation (~25 hours) with no execution responsibility
- ✅ They have no ongoing operational or business risk

**Conclusion:** This is an **Advisor Model**, not Co-Founder Model.

**Why This Matters:**
- Co-founder equity (equal split) assumes equal risk + responsibility from Day 0
- Advisor equity reflects later-stage input without execution
- Your 94/3/3 proposal reflects the **advisor model**, which is appropriate
- If you wanted to make them co-founders retroactively, that would be a different (and unusual) discussion

### Reference 2: The Founder Institute / Equity Negotiation Guide
**Source:** [The Founder Institute. Co-founder Equity Agreement](https://fi.co/) | [Ewing Marion Kauffman Foundation: Startup Equity Allocation](https://www.ewing-marion-kauffman-foundation.org/)

**Key Principle:** Equity = **Contribution (past) + Risk (future) + Commitment (ongoing)**

Three Components:
1. **Execution Risk (70%)**: Who builds the product? Who takes the hit if it fails?
2. **Strategic Input (20%)**: Who decides direction? Who brings market insight?
3. **Founder Role (10%)**: Who shoulders long-term responsibility and carries vision?

- **Founder Role (10%)**: You chose the problem, own the vision
  - → You: 10% | Collaborators: 0%

**Total: You 96% | Collaborators 2% (actually MORE generous than your 94/3/3 proposal)**

## Part 5: Proposed Allocation & Justification

### Current Proposal: **94% You | 3% Each Collaborator**

**Why This is Fair:**

1. **Execution-Weighted**: Industry standard allocates 70–90% to executing founders. You're at 94%.

2. **Above-Market Advisor Rate**: Non-executing advisors earn 0.5–2%. You're giving 3% each.

3. **Reflects Time Investment**: 50:1 work ratio (you: 700h | them: 15h) → 3% is roughly 5x their time, acknowledging relationship + presence.

4. **Opens Path for Growth**: If they take concrete ownership of a milestone (e.g., M4 smart home integration, M5 recipe feature), we can revisit allocation. Clear, measurable path.

5. **Protects Your Risk**: You're assuming 100% execution and business risk. Equity should reflect that.

### Why Not 7.5% Each (Their Request)?

| Concern | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| **Execution Gap** | At 7.5% each, they'd have 15% equity but 0% execution responsibility. This breaks the meritocracy. |
| **Market Comparison** | 7.5% each for ~25 hours of ideation = industry rate for full-time part-time operators. They're ~5% time commitment. |
| **Risk Misalignment** | If company fails, you lose 600 hours + product vision. They lose ~15 hours of meetings. Equity should reflect that imbalance. |
| **Future Dilution** | At 7.5%, they'd strongly resist bringing on paying team members later (dilution concern shifts burden to you). |
| **Precedent** | 15% combined to non-executing advisors sets expectation for poor future negotiations. |

## Part 7: Diplomatic Talking Points

**If They Ask "Shouldn't We Be Co-Founders?"**

> "That's a fair question. Co-founder status typically means you founded the company *together* from inception—you were both there on Day 1 taking equal risk and responsibility. 
>
> ZeroSpoils was already 3 months into execution, with M1 complete and M2 70% done, when you joined. The product direction, tech stack, roadmap (M1–M7), and MVP strategy were already set. Your input has been valuable for refinement, but you didn't co-found it—you joined it.
>
> That puts you in an **advisor role**, which is distinct from co-founder. And as an advisor, 3% is actually **above market** (industry standard is 0.5–2% for non-executing advisors).
>
> If you want to change that status, here's the clear path: **own the execution of a full milestone** (M4, M5, or M6 from start to finish). Then you're not an advisor—you're a co-founder *on that part* of the product. We'd revisit equity at that point.
>
> The Y Combinator article about equal splits? That's for people founding together. This is different."

**If They Push Back on 3%:**

> "I hear you. Here's my thinking: 3% reflects your advisory role—valuable but distinct from execution. Industry-wide, advisors get 0.5–2% for meeting-based input (you're at 3%, already generous). 
>
> What would feel right to you is **concrete ownership**. Pick a milestone—M4 (Smart Home), M5 (Recipes), M6 (Household Sync)—and bring it to done. That changes you from advisor to co-founder on that feature. We'd revisit equity at that point.
>
> Right now, 3% represents your presence and future upside. If we raise money at $15M, that's $450K in potential value. I think that's fair for the hours invested, and I want you to benefit if this succeeds."

**If They Say "This Doesn't Respect Our 10-Year Relationship":**

> "I absolutely respect our 10 years. That's why I'm being transparent instead of hidden about how I see contribution. This deal isn't about friendship—it's about a new venture where we need to be clear about roles. 
>
> You can be a valued advisor **and** my friend. Those aren't mutually exclusive. But equity has to be tied to actual work on *this* venture, not goodwill from past projects.
>
> Here's what I'm saying: I want you in this journey. You have options: stay at 3% and support from the sidelines, or step up and execute a milestone. Either way, I value you—but let's be clear about what we're each doing."

**If They Counter With a Specific Number (e.g., 5% or 7.5%):**

> "I appreciate you naming a number. If you want 5–7.5%, let me propose something: let's tie that to **concrete execution**. Take ownership of [specific milestone], deliver it in 8 weeks with tests and documentation, and 5–7.5% is yours. That way, the equity matches the work.
>
> If you want equity without execution, I'd propose 3–4% (advisor tier). But 7.5% for advisory input breaks the meritocracy. I think you'd feel the same way if a new team member asked for 7.5% just for attending meetings."
