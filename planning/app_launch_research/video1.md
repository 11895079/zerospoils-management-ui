### Summary

Chris, a developer focused on productivity apps, shares a detailed **framework for launching new apps**, emphasizing preparation steps and best practices that improve user engagement and product success. His process prioritizes early user interest validation, data-driven decision-making, and effective communication with users.

### Key Insights and Launch Framework

- **Pre-launch Waitlist Setup**  
  - Created before building the app to gauge interest and collect emails for beta testing.  
  - Simple landing page with one app screenshot and email collection form.  
  - Chris uses **Framer** for building waitlist pages and **FormSpark** for email integration.  
  - This waitlist serves as a springboard for initial users and feedback.

- **Analytics Implementation**  
  - Essential to install analytics early, even before having users.  
  - Helps identify reasons for user churn and informs product decisions.  
  - Chris recommends tools like **Post Hog** for tracking user behaviour inside the app.  
  - Neglecting analytics leads to uninformed development and harder problem-solving.

- **Feedback Board Integration**  
  - Enables users to leave feature requests and upvote others, revealing unanticipated user needs.  
  - Chris highlights this as the biggest lesson learned from earlier apps.  
  - Example: A list feature in his app Ellie was heavily requested despite not being planned.  
  - He uses **Canny** for a seamless user experience, with mentions of **User Jot** as an alternative.  
  - This board aids in prioritizing development to reduce churn and increase user satisfaction.

- **Email System Setup**  
  - Beyond a basic welcome email, Chris advocates for automated email sequences.  
  - These sequences can trigger based on user inactivity or timed feature highlights.  
  - Example: Ellie sends five emails over two weeks showcasing lesser-known app features to boost retention.  
  - Chris uses **Loops** for email sequencing due to its user-friendly interface and responsive support.  
  - Early setup is recommended even if not used immediately.

- **App Store Listing Optimization**  
  - Critical for iOS apps to capitalize on organic search and an initial Apple boost after launch.  
  - Key elements include:  
    - High-quality app store screenshots (recommended 3–4 days of work).  
    - Strong app title, description, and relevant tags.  
  - Good screenshots improve first impressions and download rates.  
  - Apple provides a temporary boost in visibility post-launch, making preparation vital.

- **Landing Page Development**  
  - Separate from the waitlist page; designed to showcase the app and drive downloads.  
  - Typically requires about a week of focused effort.  
  - Assets from app store screenshots can be reused.  
  - Also built in Framer, with an emphasis on good copy and presentation.  
  - Launch should not be delayed excessively for the perfect page, but quality matters.

### Launch Timing and Strategy

- **When to Launch**  
  - Determined by confidence in user retention based on beta test data.  
  - Key indicator: users sticking around for multiple days rather than dropping off immediately.  
  - No exact numeric threshold; relies on intuition guided by analytics.

- **Launch Execution**  
  - Chris’s personal approach is modest: announcing on social media, YouTube, Twitter, LinkedIn, and emailing the waitlist.  
  - He acknowledges this is not an aggressive marketing strategy but aligns with his comfort level.  
  - For a bigger launch, recommended tactics include:  
    - Starting promotion 4 weeks prior.  
    - Leveraging product hunt launches, social media shares, friends’ networks.  
    - Engaging online communities such as Reddit.  
  - The goal is to maximize exposure to the app store and landing pages.

- **Data-driven decision making** via early analytics setup.  
- **User-centric development** informed by transparent feedback collection.  
- **Retention-focused user communication** through automated email sequences.  
- **Strategic app store and landing page optimisation** to maximise visibility and downloads.  
- **Flexible launch timing** based on user retention confidence rather than fixed metrics.
